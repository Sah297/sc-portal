Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Audi Mexico is in acute distress following multiple production halts costing $4m per day with executive-confirmed financial impact. The October-November 2025 blockades exposed catastrophic vulnerability in their just-in-time model forcing indefinite suspensions and tariff penalties. With management publicly seeking solutions and 146k annual unit output at risk, this is a high-urgency transformation opportunity.

Signals to Lead With

→ Quantified daily disruption cost — Audi management confirmed to industry association that each production day lost costs $4m across three shifts with $1.3m per shift operational cost.
→ Repeated multi-day production suspensions — Plant suspended operations October 30-31 and again November 24-25, 2025 due to parts shortages from transport blockades affecting 5,200 workers.
→ Sole-source global production risk — San Jose Chiapa is the only plant producing Q5 for global markets except China, amplifying supply chain fragility for 146,784 annual units.
→ Margin deterioration under operational stress — Audi Group operating margin compressed from 6% in FY2024 to 5.1% in FY2025 representing 90 bps decline on €65.5bn revenue.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Transform from fragile just-in-time to resilient just-in-case with segmented inventory strategy and integrated S&OP across 150k unit capacity.
• Platform (Kinaxis RapidResponse): Kinaxis RapidResponse enables real-time supply chain visibility and scenario modeling to anticipate disruptions and dynamically adjust production schedules across Mexico-global network.
• Planning as a Service: Accenture PaaS manages daily tactical planning and disruption response while Audi focuses on Q5 ramp and electrification roadmap.
• AI & Analytics: AI predicts transport disruption patterns from social/weather data and optimizes safety stock positioning across tier 1-3 supplier network.
