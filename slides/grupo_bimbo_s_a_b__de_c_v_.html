Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Grupo Bimbo is in active transformation with North America margins bottoming at 5.9% in Q4 2024 before recovering to 9% in Q2 2025, signaling early investment payback. With net debt/EBITDA at 2.9x (up from 2.1x) funding capex and acquisitions, the company urgently needs to sweat existing assets and unlock working capital to fund growth without further leverage. The MXN 7bn working capital spike and 390bps margin compression create a burning platform for planning excellence.

Signals to Lead With

→ Margin recovery trajectory underway — North America EBITDA margin recovered from 5.9% in Q4 2024 to 9% in Q2 2025, demonstrating receptivity to transformation investments and early payback.
→ Leverage constraint on growth — Net debt/EBITDA increased to 2.9x from 2.1x as company funded capex and 5 acquisitions in 2024, creating urgency to unlock internal cash.
→ Working capital deterioration — Working capital increased MXN 7 billion primarily from inventory and receivables, signaling immediate optimization opportunity worth $350m+.
→ Active supply chain transformation — Company investing heavily in value chain transformation and closed 4 bakeries in 2024, indicating openness to strategic supply chain partners and platform investments.
