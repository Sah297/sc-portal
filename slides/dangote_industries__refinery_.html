Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Dangote Refinery represents an exceptional buying signal: a $20bn mega-asset operating at 26-31% utilization due to planning failures, carrying $3.65bn debt requiring IPO restructuring in Aug 2026, and needing $1.0-1.5bn monthly working capital for feedstock. The refinery is Africa's largest with 650k bpd capacity but chronically supply-constrained, creating immediate ROI for planning transformation. With planned 2026 IPO and expansion to 1.4m bpd by 2028, this is a narrow window to embed Accenture before institutionalization.

Signals to Lead With

→ Liquidity crisis driving $5bn IPO — Refinery carrying $3.65bn debt with Fitch downgrade to B+(nga) in Aug 2024 due to 50% utilization and ₦2.7tn FX loss, necessitating Aug 2026 IPO for balance sheet relief.
→ Operational capacity severely underutilized — Operating at 26-31% capacity (5-6m barrel monthly allocations vs 19.5m needed) despite 650k bpd nameplate and ability to reach 99.4% utilization with proper feedstock planning.
→ Working capital trapped in procurement — Requires $1.0-1.5bn monthly for 13-19 crude cargoes with no predictive planning, creating cash cycle pressure that diverts funds from debt servicing to feedstock purchases.
→ Expansion without resolving core issues — Announced $350m expansion to 1.4m bpd by 2028 and signed XCMG equipment deal in Feb 2026 while still unable to secure stable crude supply at current 650k capacity.
