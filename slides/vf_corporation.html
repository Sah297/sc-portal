Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

VF is in acute distress with years of Vietnam supply chain investments now undermined by 20% US tariffs effective July 2025. The company spent $105M on Reinvent transformation in FY24, saw gross margin compress 120bps to 48.4%, and carries $5.3B net debt while revenue declined 4% to $9.5B. This is a perfect storm requiring immediate re-optimization of their Asia-heavy sourcing footprint across Vietnam, Cambodia, and Indonesia with urgency to preserve margins and prevent further cash burn.

→ Active $105M Reinvent transformation in flight — VF spent $105M in FY24 on Reinvent program and continues restructuring, indicating active investment in supply chain and cost optimization with executive mandate for change.
→ Vietnam tariff creates existential sourcing crisis — VF expanded heavily into Vietnam to exit China; new 20% tariff effective July 2025 eliminates cost advantage and forces immediate multi-country sourcing re-optimization across entire portfolio.
→ Gross margin in freefall needing immediate recovery — Gross margin fell 120bps YoY to 48.4% in Q4 FY24 driven by promotional pressure and restructuring, with management guiding continued margin pressure requiring planning capability upgrade.
→ $5.3B net debt creating cash urgency — Despite generating $804M free cash flow in FY24, VF carries $5.3B net debt and explicitly prioritizes debt reduction, making working capital optimization and margin recovery financially critical.
