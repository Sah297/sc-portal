Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

CVS is in active restructuring mode with new CEO David Joyner (Q4 2024), 1,170 store closures completed or planned, and urgent need to stabilize margins and cash flow. The combination of operational distress, platform modernization opportunity (Kinaxis), and $357.8bn scale creates immediate opening for large-scale planning transformation to support turnaround.

Signals to Lead With

→ Leadership transition with restructuring mandate — New CEO David Joyner assumed role in Q4 2024 with explicit mandate to improve margins and reduce Medicare Advantage membership by high single-digits in 2025.
→ Severe margin compression requiring intervention — Adjusted operating income declined $1.5bn in Q4 2024 YoY with Health Care Benefits segment showing 340bps MBR deterioration to 89.6%.
→ Massive network rationalization underway — 1,170 stores closed 2022-2025 representing 11% of footprint, requiring supply chain reconfiguration and inventory rebalancing across remaining locations.
→ Cash generation crisis — Operating cash flow declined $4.3bn in 2024 to approximately $9-10bn, with 2025 guidance signaling continued pressure requiring working capital optimization.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Integrated S&OP across pharmacy, consumer wellness, and PBM operations to optimize store network footprint and inventory positioning amid ongoing closures.
• Platform (Kinaxis RapidResponse): RapidResponse enables real-time scenario planning for store rationalization, demand sensing across 9,000+ locations, and concurrent supply chain optimization during restructuring.
• Planning as a Service: PaaS model accelerates transformation during leadership transition and operational restructuring without adding permanent headcount to constrained cost structure.
• AI & Analytics: AI-driven demand sensing for prescription and front-store volumes plus network optimization algorithms to right-size inventory across shrinking footprint and improve cash conversion.
