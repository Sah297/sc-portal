Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

URBN faces acute pressure from 50% India tariffs and early inventory pull-forward strategy that risks fashion misses and excess stock. With inventory up 12.9% versus 7.7% sales growth, and management explicitly calling out 75 bps margin drag despite recent improvement, the company needs immediate planning capabilities to model tariff scenarios, optimize dual-sourcing, and reduce working capital exposure while maintaining fashion agility.

Signals to Lead With

→ Tariff margin compression despite mitigation — 75 bps Q4 margin drag from tariffs even after vendor negotiations and sourcing shifts, with India 50% tariff forcing line-by-line sourcing reset.
→ Inventory velocity mismatch creating WC risk — Inventory grew 12.9% ($70.9m increase) while sales grew 7.7%, with comparable inventory up 11.3% creating $75m+ working capital opportunity.
→ Lead time extension threatening fashion accuracy — Air-to-ocean shift adds 30 days transit time, forcing early fall procurement with CEO acknowledging 'fashion might not be as accurate as we would like.'
→ Multi-country sourcing complexity escalating — India-Vietnam-Turkey diversification with dual-sourcing on most own-brand products requires sophisticated landed cost and scenario modeling capabilities.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Integrate tariff scenario planning into S&OP to balance early procurement, fashion risk, and margin protection across multi-country sourcing.
• Platform (o9 Solutions): o9 enables real-time tariff cost modeling, dual-sourcing optimization, and landed cost visibility needed for URBN's India-Vietnam-Turkey diversification strategy.
• Planning as a Service: Deploy managed planning services to execute vendor term negotiations, country-of-origin shifts, and transportation mode optimization at scale across three segments.
• AI & Analytics: AI-driven demand sensing reduces 30-day fashion risk from ocean freight shift while markdown optimization protects margins amid early inventory receipts.
