Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

JD Sports is in acute distress with FY27 profit guidance of £750-850m representing a 12-18% decline from FY26's already-depressed £852m. Management faces a perfect storm: 120bps margin compression, £30m regulatory cost increases, persistent negative LFL sales (-2.3% in Q1 FY27), and subdued market outlook. The company's FY26 results show planning dysfunction—strong top-line growth failing to convert to profit—creating immediate urgency for supply chain transformation to control costs and restore margins.
