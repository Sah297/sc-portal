Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

JD Sports faces a perfect storm of operational and financial pressure requiring immediate planning transformation. The £30m regulatory cost hit, revised profit guidance downward by £40-100m, and acknowledged inventory visibility issues create urgent need for supply chain optimization. With 181 new stores opened in FY25 and continued expansion, scalable planning capabilities are critical to protect margins and working capital.

Signals to Lead With

→ Profit guidance downgraded from £955-1,035m to £915-935m — Management explicitly revised FY25 profit expectations downward, signaling immediate pressure to find operational efficiencies and cost savings beyond the £30m regulatory impact.
→ Inventory accuracy issues publicly acknowledged — Stock levels not accurately reflected online causing customer frustration indicates system gaps requiring technology and process intervention to protect revenue.
→ £30m fixed cost increase from wage/NI changes — Regulatory-driven cost inflation with no revenue offset creates existential need for planning optimization to protect operating margins in promotional market.
→ Aggressive expansion with 4,500+ stores and acquisitions — 181 new JD stores opened in FY25 plus Hibbett and Courir acquisitions demand scalable, integrated planning platform to manage complexity at speed.

• Planning as a Service: Rapid deployment of PaaS model addresses immediate inventory accuracy and demand planning gaps while building internal capability during o9 implementation.
