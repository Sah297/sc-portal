Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Lilly faces urgent need for advanced supply chain capabilities after $400m Q4 2024 revenue miss and two-year tirzepatide shortage that spawned parallel compounding market. With 32% revenue growth projected for 2025 ($58-61bn guidance) and 1.6x incretin production increase, the company requires sophisticated demand sensing and capacity planning now to avoid repeating costly supply-demand mismatches.

Signals to Lead With

→ Documented $400m revenue shortfall Q4 2024 — Q4 2024 revenue missed low-end guidance by $400m due to demand-supply imbalances and channel inventory volatility per January 2025 SEC filing.
→ Two-year drug shortage resolved October 2024 — Tirzepatide on FDA shortage list since late 2022, only resolved October 2024 after billions in manufacturing investment per FDA announcement.
→ Massive production scale-up underway — Company producing 1.6x incretin doses in H1 2025 vs H1 2024, requiring sophisticated capacity planning to synchronize with demand.
→ Parallel compounding market threat — Shortage enabled compounding pharmacies to create competing tirzepatide products, eroding Lilly's brand control and market share through 2024.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Integrated S&OP linking prescribing data, payer reimbursement dynamics, and manufacturing ramp-up to prevent demand-supply mismatches and channel inventory volatility.
• Platform (Kinaxis RapidResponse): RapidResponse concurrent planning enables real-time demand sensing across prescriber, payer, and channel data with scenario modeling for capacity expansion decisions.
• Planning as a Service: PaaS manages complex multi-signal demand forecasting (prescriptions, payer coverage, wholesaler patterns) and rapid manufacturing scale-up coordination.
• AI & Analytics: AI agents predict tirzepatide demand from prescribing trends and payer formulary changes while optimizing manufacturing network allocation across indications.
