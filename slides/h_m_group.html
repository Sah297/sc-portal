Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

H&M is at an inflection point: logistics transformation completed, capital shift to tech underway, and active supplier base consolidation to address tariff risks. The company explicitly acknowledged demand forecasting gaps causing stock-outs and is exploring AI for supply chain, creating immediate need for advanced planning capabilities. With inventory already down 12% but sales constrained, the tension between efficiency and growth is acute.

Signals to Lead With

→ Strategic shift away from supply chain investments — February 2026 announcement shifting $1bn capex from supply chain to stores/tech after completing logistics transformation, creating planning capability gap.
→ Supplier consolidation underway — H&M consolidated from wider factory base to 800 suppliers across 23 countries as of Feb 2026, requiring sophisticated network planning.
→ China sourcing diversification driven by tariffs — Q4 2025 tariffs caused 200bps margin pressure; company moving production to nearshore suppliers in Central America and Turkey to mitigate future impacts.
→ CEO acknowledges demand planning weakness — Q2 2026: CEO stated tighter inventory affected ability to meet demand and noted room to improve demand forecasting and stock availability.
