Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Dangote Refinery is in acute distress with 74% unutilized capacity due to crude supply constraints and a live legal battle with NNPC over allocations. Operating at 26-31% vs 650,000 bpd capacity while paying $18/barrel premiums on imports creates urgent need for supply chain optimization. With a planned September 2026 IPO and expansion to 1.4m bpd by 2028, the window for transformation is now.

Signals to Lead With

→ Critical capacity underutilization — Refinery operates at 26-31% capacity (receives 7 cargoes vs 19 needed monthly), leaving 74% capacity idle and revenue unrealized.
→ Active legal dispute with NNPC — April 2026 lawsuit filed challenging crude allocations and import licenses, with NNPC counter-filing claiming refinery cannot prove supply adequacy.
→ Import premium bleeding margin — Forced to import crude at $18/barrel premium above Brent benchmarks due to insufficient domestic allocations, directly eroding profitability.
→ Pre-IPO transformation urgency — Planned September 2026 IPO at London and Nigerian exchanges creates 4-month window to stabilize operations and demonstrate supply chain resilience to investors.
