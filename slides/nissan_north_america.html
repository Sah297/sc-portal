Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Nissan faces a crisis trifecta: dealer oil rationing through mid-2027, 0.6% operating margin (down from 5-6%), and $14.2bn inventory (+12.5%) amid declining sales. The company issued allocation letters, reported negative $243bn automotive FCF, and is executing turnaround under new CEO following failed Honda merger. This creates urgent need for supply chain transformation with Board-level visibility.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active dealer rationing program — Nissan cut 0W-20 oil allocations to 55% and 5W-30 to 70% starting May 21, 2026 with prioritization bulletins issued to all dealers for warranty/recall vs retail customers.
→ Operating margin at 14-year low — FY2024 operating margin collapsed to 0.6% (69.8bn yen profit on 12.6tn yen revenue) with negative 215.9bn yen automotive operating loss and negative 242.8bn yen free cash flow.
→ CEO transition during turnaround — New CEO Ivan Espinosa appointed April 1, 2025 following failed Honda merger and $4.5bn net loss, launching 'Re:Nissan' recovery plan in May 2025.
→ Inventory-sales mismatch — Inventory increased 12.5% to $14.2bn while global retail sales declined 2.8% to 3.35m units, indicating $1.4bn+ working capital opportunity from network optimization.
