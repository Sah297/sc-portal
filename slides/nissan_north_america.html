Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Nissan is in active crisis mode executing the Re:Nissan recovery plan with ¥500bn cost reduction, 7 plant closures, and 20,000 job cuts by FY2027. The Morelos plant closure and production consolidation to Aguascalientes create immediate planning complexity requiring external expertise. With negative automotive free cash flow, 0.6% operating margins, and a mandate to achieve profitability by FY2026, the urgency and budget for transformation are at peak levels.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active restructuring program with clear targets — Re:Nissan plan launched May 2025 targeting ¥500bn cost savings, ¥250bn fixed and ¥250bn variable, with explicit automotive profitability goal by FY2026.
→ Major network consolidation underway — Consolidating from 17 to 10 plants globally by FY2027, with Morelos CIVAC closure by March 2026 transferring all production to Aguascalientes—requiring immediate planning transformation.
→ Severe cash and profitability distress — ¥242.8bn negative automotive free cash flow in FY2024, ¥670.9bn net loss, and operating margin collapsed to 0.6% from 4.5% prior year—creating burning platform for change.
→ Existing Kinaxis customer with expansion potential — Already on Kinaxis RapidResponse platform, providing lower-risk expansion opportunity for enhanced modules, AI capabilities, and managed services versus greenfield implementation.
