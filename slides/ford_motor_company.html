Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Ford faces acute supply chain crisis with $1.5-2bn Novelis aluminum impact hitting their most profitable product (F-150) while gross margins turned negative in Q4 2025. The company suspended guidance, is sourcing aluminum globally at premium cost, and lost 100,000 F-Series units—creating urgent need for multi-tier visibility, scenario planning, and mix optimization that Kinaxis + Accenture can deliver immediately.

Signals to Lead With

→ Material supplier single-point-of-failure realized — Novelis fires caused 100,000 unit loss and $1.5-2bn impact; Ford sourcing alternative aluminum offshore at premium, exposing lack of dual-source strategy and supplier risk visibility.
→ Guidance suspension due to supply uncertainty — Ford suspended FY2025 financial guidance in May 2025 due to tariff and Novelis uncertainty, signaling loss of planning control and need for scenario-based S&OP platform.
→ Inventory elevated 22% above median despite shortages — Inventory at 38 days ($15.3bn), 22% above historical median of 31 days, indicating poor allocation and working capital trapped in wrong products/locations during F-150 shortage.
→ Negative gross margin driven by material cost — Gross margin fell to -0.27% in Q4 2025 from prior positive levels, reflecting lost pricing power and inability to absorb $2.5bn tariff/aluminum costs without real-time total-cost-to-serve planning.

• Planning Opportunities: Integrated S&OP linking supplier capacity constraints, material availability, and product mix optimization to maximize margin given F-150 aluminum constraints and tariff scenarios.
• Platform (Kinaxis RapidResponse): Kinaxis RapidResponse enables concurrent multi-tier supply visibility, what-if scenario planning for tariff/supplier disruptions, and real-time allocation of constrained aluminum to highest-margin F-150 trims.
