Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

GM faces a perfect storm: $7.2bn EV writedowns, 200 bps margin erosion from tariffs and cost inflation, and 70-100% DRAM price increases threatening premium vehicle production. With inventory at $15.3bn, suspended buybacks, and 2025 guidance under reassessment, CFO publicly cited unprecedented cost escalation. This creates urgent executive-level demand for scenario-based planning, supplier risk mitigation, and cost optimization capabilities that Accenture + Kinaxis deliver.

Signals to Lead With

→ Massive EV capacity writedown — GM took $7.2bn special charges in Q4 2025 realigning EV capacity, signaling urgent need for demand-driven capacity planning.
→ Margin compression under cost pressure — Gross margin fell to 6.27% from 8.4% YoY with EBIT-adjusted margin down 200 bps to 7.9% in Q1 2025 amid DRAM, labor, warranty cost escalation.
→ Guidance suspended amid uncertainty — GM suspended 2025 guidance updates and $6bn share buyback program in April 2025, citing tariff uncertainty and cost visibility gaps.
→ DRAM shortage escalating rapidly — Automotive DRAM prices up 70% YoY with 70-100% further increases expected 2026, directly impacting GM's premium cockpit and ADAS systems through 2027.
