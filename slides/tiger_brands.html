Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Tiger Brands presents a compelling opportunity following its successful turnaround delivering 220bps margin expansion and R1.2bn working capital release in FY25. The company's ongoing struggle with pricing power constraints (price deflation of 0.8%) and private label pressure, combined with a refreshed federated operating model and strong cash position (R3.2bn), creates immediate need for advanced planning capabilities to sustain margin gains while navigating competitive intensity and uneven premiumization progress.

Signals to Lead With

→ Margin expansion momentum at risk — Gross margin improved 220bps to 31.3% in FY25, but ongoing pricing power constraints and private label competition threaten sustainability of gains without sophisticated planning tools.
→ Working capital optimization in focus — Company generated R1.2bn working capital inflow in FY25 with 90% cash conversion ratio, demonstrating management focus on operational efficiency and cash generation creating receptivity to planning solutions.
→ Volume recovery priority post-turnaround — Volumes recovered from -6% decline in FY24 to +3.5% growth in FY25, signaling strategic pivot to volume-led growth requiring demand-driven planning and portfolio mix optimization.
→ Portfolio optimization underway — Company completed R4.3bn in non-core disposals (Carozzi, Baby Wellbeing) and implemented federated operating model, indicating openness to strategic transformation and planning infrastructure investment.
