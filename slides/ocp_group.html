Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

OCP presents an urgent, high-value opportunity as the world's largest phosphate producer ($11.4B revenue) faces existential supply chain risk from Strait of Hormuz disruption blocking 3.7M tons annual sulphur imports. With fertilizer prices surging 30% and OCP advancing plant maintenance due to feedstock constraints, the company requires immediate supply chain transformation to protect margins and maintain production through 2027. Accenture's combination of Kinaxis implementation, geopolitical scenario planning, and multi-sourcing strategy directly addresses OCP's stated priorities around industrial flexibility and cost competitiveness amid $13B green investment program.

Signals to Lead With

→ Critical raw material supply disruption — OCP depends on 3.7M metric tons sulphur annually from Persian Gulf; 44% of global seaborne sulphur trade blocked through Hormuz with inventory coverage only through July 2026.
→ Accelerated maintenance due to supply constraints — OCP advanced plant maintenance at fertilizer facilities in response to tight sulphur/ammonia availability; World Bank confirms OCP accelerated maintenance likely due to feedstock market disruptions.
→ Production capacity expansion under pressure — OCP invested $910M in Q4 2025 capex targeting 20M tonnes capacity by 2027, but cannot scale production to replace Gulf supply without securing alternative sulphur sources.
→ Strategic government recognition of supply criticality — US President Trump issued emergency proclamation June 29, 2026 suspending tariffs on Moroccan fertilizers, citing OCP access as critical to American food supply amid global shortage.
