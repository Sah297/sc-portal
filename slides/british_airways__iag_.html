Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

BA faces acute pain from Rolls-Royce Trent 1000 supply chain disruptions that will persist through 2026, forcing cancellations and route suspensions despite record profitability. With 15% of 787 fleet grounded and routes cut by 51-75%, BA urgently needs supplier visibility and constraint-based planning capabilities. The airline's £7bn transformation program and strong cash generation position them to invest in planning modernization to protect margins and operational performance.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active crisis mode with grounded fleet — BA grounded 6 of 40 Boeing 787s (15% fleet) and suspended London Gatwick-JFK route December 2024-March 2025 due to Rolls-Royce parts shortage.
→ Multi-year problem with no quick fix — Rolls-Royce confirmed supply chain constraints will continue through 2026, and BA publicly stated they do not believe the issue will be solved quickly.
→ Operational excellence is strategic priority — BA's Net Promoter Score improved 10.5 points in 2025 with 70% driven by reliability; engine disruptions directly threaten this strategic achievement.
→ Strong financial position to invest — IAG generated €6.4bn operating cash flow in 2024 and €3.6bn free cash flow, with 15.1% operating margin and plans to return €1bn to shareholders.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Integrate supplier risk visibility with S&OP to anticipate Rolls-Royce part delays and dynamically replan route capacity 12-24 weeks ahead.
• Platform (Blue Yonder): Blue Yonder enables multi-tier supplier visibility and constraint-based planning to model engine availability scenarios and optimize fleet deployment.
• Planning as a Service: Accenture PaaS provides ongoing critical parts planning and supplier collaboration to manage Rolls-Royce constraints through 2026.
