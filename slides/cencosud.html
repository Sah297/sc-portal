Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Cencosud faces acute competitive pressure from Mercado Libre's operational excellence (50% same/next-day delivery in Q1 2025 vs traditional retail) while managing format obsolescence evidenced by 77 store closures in 2025. The May 2023 partnership with Mercado Libre has not delivered expected execution results, with net income down 20% to $248m despite 15.9% revenue growth. With $610m capex investment planned for 2025 and hypermarket-to-convenience transformation underway, Cencosud needs immediate supply chain planning capabilities to compete digitally and rationalize its 1,045-store multi-country portfolio. This is a strong buying signal for Accenture: existing Blue Yonder platform provides tech anchor, active transformation underway creates urgency, and multi-country complexity requires enterprise-scale consulting support.
