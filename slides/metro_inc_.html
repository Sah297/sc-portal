Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Metro is in acute pain mode with a live 9-week strike driving visible shelf gaps, $2m weekly cost bleed, and projected 4% Q3 EPS decline—creating board-level urgency ahead of August earnings. Combined with recent $30.6m DC failure and multi-year automation investment drag, management faces investor pressure to demonstrate supply chain resilience and margin recovery, making this a high-urgency, high-value intervention window before Q3 close in July.
