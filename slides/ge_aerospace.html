Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

GE Aerospace presents an urgent opportunity with clear financial distress signals and executive visibility on supply chain as a strategic bottleneck. The company achieved record revenue growth and margin expansion despite 10% engine delivery reduction, indicating massive pent-up demand if production constraints are resolved. With CFM targeting 2,000+ LEAP deliveries in 2026 and inventory days ballooning to 150, leadership needs immediate supply chain transformation to capture $15.5B order backlog.

Signals to Lead With

→ Quantified production shortfall — LEAP engine deliveries fell 10% YoY (1,407 vs 1,570 units) despite record $15.5B orders, creating delivery gap and customer dissatisfaction.
→ Working capital deterioration — Cash conversion cycle expanded to 172 days in 2025 from 164 days in 2024, with inventory processing period reaching 150 days vs 107 days in 2021.
→ Publicly acknowledged supply chain crisis — CEO and CFO cited material inputs and priority supplier site constraints on earnings calls, with 26% increase in material inputs needed in H2 2024.
→ Aggressive growth targets amid constraints — Company targeting 2,000+ LEAP deliveries in 2026 and high-teens revenue growth in 2025 while currently constrained by supplier bottlenecks and production challenges.
