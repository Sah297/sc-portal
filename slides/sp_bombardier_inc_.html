Accenture S&P Solutions

  • Spend Optimization — Bombardier's $10bn revenue base implies $6-7bn in direct materials spend (engines, avionics, structures, raw materials) and $1.5-2bn in indirect spend (MRO, facilities, IT, professional services). Critical opportunities include: (1) Strategic sourcing for engines, avionics, and structural components with should-cost analytics; (2) Supplier consolidation from 5,900 to optimized base; (3) Indirect spend category management across 49 US states and 35 countries; (4) Negotiation leverage improvement through fact-based should-cost modeling; (5) Total cost optimization including quality, delivery, and risk-adjusted pricing.
  • Procurement Reinvention — Transform Bombardier's procurement organization from reactive firefighting to strategic value creation. Key initiatives: (1) Redesign operating model to reduce 950-person procurement team span of control; (2) Shift from field deployment (50+ resources) to centralized intelligence and automation; (3) Implement category management structure aligned to direct/indirect spend; (4) Deploy supplier segmentation strategy (strategic, preferred, transactional); (5) Build procurement Centers of Excellence for risk, analytics, and supplier development; (6) Enable demand-driven supply planning to eliminate out-of-sequence production work.
  • Supplier Risk & Resiliency — Mission-critical given current crisis. Bombardier's single-source engine supplier issues causing weeks of assembly line stoppages represents existential delivery risk. Key initiatives: (1) Implement multi-tier supply chain mapping and real-time visibility into Tier 2-3 suppliers; (2) Deploy early warning system with predictive analytics for supplier financial health, quality, and delivery performance; (3) Develop dual-sourcing strategy for critical components (engines, avionics) to eliminate single points of failure; (4) Create supplier development programs to improve conformity rates and reduce scrap; (5) Build supplier resilience scorecards with proactive intervention protocols; (6) Establish alternative supply scenarios and business continuity plans; (7) Implement quality management system integration with key suppliers to prevent non-conformities.
  • AI-Intelligent Procurement — Deploy SynOps AI-powered platform and ATLAS control tower to transform reactive procurement into predictive, autonomous operations. Key use cases: (1) ATLAS Control Tower providing real-time visibility across 5,900 suppliers with predictive disruption alerts; (2) Autonomous agents monitoring engine supplier quality metrics and triggering interventions before non-conformities occur; (3) AI-powered demand forecasting integrated with supplier capacity to eliminate parts shortages (currently 500 items); (4) Intelligent sourcing agents automating RFx processes and supplier evaluation across indirect spend categories; (5) Procurement co-pilot providing decision support for field resources and category managers; (6) Natural language processing for contract intelligence and risk clause identification; (7) Machine learning models predicting supplier delivery performance and recommending inventory buffers.
