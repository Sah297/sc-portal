Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

HelloFresh is in active transformation mode with 80% of €300m efficiency program deployed, distribution center consolidation completed in 2024-2025, and market exits from Spain/Italy. The company faces continued revenue pressure (-9% in FY2025, guidance of -6% to -3% for 2026) while managing 2,000+ suppliers across volatile fresh food supply chains. With meal kit margins recovered to 13.5% but RTE at -1.2% and operational complexity still high, there's urgent need for planning modernization and AI-driven supplier risk management.

Signals to Lead With

→ Major efficiency program 80% complete — HelloFresh deployed €240m of €300m efficiency reset by end-2025, targeting labor productivity, procurement savings, and DC rationalization with final €60m pending.
→ Network centralization just completed — Company closed distribution centers in UK, Georgia, and Texas in 2024-2025 and exited Spain/Italy markets, creating need to optimize remaining network.
→ Margin recovery amid volume decline — Meal kit AEBITDA margin improved from 9.8% to 13.5% while revenue fell 9%, indicating efficiency gains but continued pressure requiring next-level optimization.
→ 2000+ supplier complexity with disruptions — Fresh food supply base facing climate and geopolitical volatility with constantly changing product base making long-term forecasting difficult per company statements.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: S&OP transformation to align meal kit product innovation with supply capacity post-centralization and improve demand visibility across constantly changing SKU base.
• Platform (Blue Yonder): Blue Yonder provides demand sensing and supplier collaboration capabilities critical for managing 2,000+ suppliers with volatile fresh ingredient flows and weekly menu changes.

• AI & Analytics: AI agents enable real-time demand pattern recognition across changing menu offerings and early warning for supplier disruptions from climate/geopolitical events.
