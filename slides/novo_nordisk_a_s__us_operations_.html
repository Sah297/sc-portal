Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Novo Nordisk presents exceptional urgency with $9bn capex commitment for 2025 and ongoing supply constraints threatening growth in a 41% expanding obesity segment. The $11.7bn Catalent acquisition creates immediate integration complexity requiring sophisticated planning capabilities, while maintaining 84.7% gross margins under massive scaling pressure demands best-in-class supply chain transformation.

Signals to Lead With

→ Massive capital deployment underway — Company committed $9bn capex for 2025 (up from $6.3bn in 2024) plus $11.7bn Catalent acquisition, creating urgent need for capacity planning optimization.
→ Acknowledged supply chain distress — Management explicitly cites continued periodic supply constraints and drug shortage notifications across products and geographies in 2025 outlook.
→ Network complexity explosion — Integration of three Catalent fill-finish sites (Italy, Belgium, Indiana) with 3,000+ employees into existing network while maintaining cold chain integrity.
→ Margin protection imperative — Need to maintain industry-leading 84.7% gross margin while scaling manufacturing creates demand for advanced S&OP and scenario planning capabilities.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Integrated S&OP across API production, fill-finish, and cold chain distribution to eliminate periodic supply constraints and align $9bn capex phasing with demand.
• Platform (Kinaxis RapidResponse): RapidResponse enables concurrent planning for temperature-sensitive multi-stage production with what-if capacity scenarios to manage Catalent integration and brownfield expansions.
• Planning as a Service: Accenture-led planning operations can manage complex multi-site capacity allocation and cold chain optimization while Novo scales internal teams.
• AI & Analytics: AI agents predict GLP-1 demand volatility and optimize fill-finish site allocation across Catalent integration to minimize shortages and protect 84.7% margins.
