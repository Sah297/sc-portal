Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

ASML faces acute supply chain tension with €38.8bn backlog constrained by supplier capacity and geopolitical uncertainty threatening 20% of revenue. Company publicly acknowledges supply chain capacity, timely parts availability, and tariff pressure as key risks for 2025-2026, while simultaneously targeting 25% production increase in 2026. This creates immediate need for advanced planning capabilities to manage complex supplier network, scenario-plan export restrictions, and optimize constrained production capacity.

Signals to Lead With

→ Record €38.8bn backlog with finite production capacity — ASML's backlog represents years of committed revenue but company faces physical limits in scaling production, creating constraint-based planning urgency.
→ 20% revenue at risk from geopolitical restrictions — China sales represent 20% of 2025 revenue with MATCH Act restrictions potentially materializing, requiring scenario planning and demand reallocation capabilities.
→ 25% shipment increase target for 2026 — ASML plans to ship 60 EUV systems in 2026 (up from 48 in 2025), requiring supplier capacity optimization across 5,000+ supplier network.
→ Critical supplier bottlenecks acknowledged publicly — High-NA optics, light sources, and precision mechatronics from single-source suppliers cited as capacity constraints extending lead times.
