Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Galp faces urgent supply chain resilience requirements following the April 28 blackout that exposed critical infrastructure dependencies. With 60% of suppliers experiencing payment delays and €711m in stabilization costs, management needs immediate planning capability to diversify aviation fuel sourcing and optimize working capital amid Portugal's €400m grid investment program through 2026.

Signals to Lead With

→ Acute infrastructure crisis trigger — April 28, 2025 Iberian blackout created first-ever full system failure requiring black start recovery and exposing aviation fuel supply vulnerabilities.
→ Cascading supplier financial stress — 60% of Portugal-based suppliers hit by payment delays with B2B gaps expanding from 16 to 20 days, creating €581m working capital pressure.
→ Major capital commitments to resilience — Portugal investing €400m in grid upgrades with 750MW battery storage by 2026, requiring Galp to adapt supply chain to new energy infrastructure.
→ Executive visibility and urgency — Q4 2025 results highlighted €174m inventory effects and working capital builds as CEO emphasized operational resilience for 2026 guidance.
