Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Target represents an urgent, high-value opportunity with new CEO Michael Fiddelke announcing the most significant transformation in a decade including a $6bn turnaround plan and explicit focus on operational discipline. Four consecutive quarters of sales declines, inventory bloat of $15.2bn (up 27.5%), and 100 bps gross margin erosion create immediate burning platform for supply chain and planning capabilities. Existing Blue Yonder platform provides direct implementation pathway with low switching cost.

Signals to Lead With

→ New CEO turnaround mandate — CEO Michael Fiddelke appointed August 2025 with explicit $6bn transformation plan and multiyear acceleration office focused on operational speed and agility.
→ Inventory crisis requiring immediate action — Inventory jumped from $11.9bn to $15.2bn (27.5% increase) by November 2024 while sales declined, indicating severe demand forecasting and planning breakdown.
→ Margin compression from promotional ineffectiveness — Gross margin contracted 100 bps in Q2 FY25 (30.0% to 29.0%) driven by higher promotional markdowns and clearance activity signaling broken price/promotion management.
→ Market share loss acceleration — Comparable sales declined 3.8% in Q1, 1.9% in Q2, and 2.7% in Q3 FY2025 with store traffic down 5.7%, representing sustained volume erosion versus Walmart.

• Planning Opportunities: Implement integrated S&OP to connect merchandising decisions with demand signals and inventory positioning across 2,000 stores.
• Platform (Blue Yonder): Blue Yonder demand forecasting and inventory optimization directly addresses Target's $3.3bn excess inventory and promotional markdown challenges.
