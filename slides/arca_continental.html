Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Arca Continental is experiencing acute margin compression at a regulatory inflection point, with Q4 2025 net income down 11.5% marking their first decline in five years. The doubling of IEPS tax to 3.08 pesos/liter creates immediate urgency for advanced planning capabilities to model price-pack-channel scenarios and optimize promotional spend. Management explicitly acknowledged volume declines in 2026 guidance, signaling readiness for transformation investment.

Signals to Lead With

→ First net income decline in 5 years — Q4 2025 net income dropped 11.5% to 4.66bn pesos despite 30% revenue growth, indicating structural margin pressure requiring intervention.
→ IEPS tax nearly doubled in 2026 — Tax increased from 1.64 to 3.08 pesos per liter, with new levy on sugar-free products creating $200m+ annual impact requiring scenario planning.
→ Management acknowledges 2026 volume decline — CEO confirmed volume pressure in Mexico already factored into projections, signaling acceptance of market headwinds and need for mitigation tools.
→ EBITDA margin at 8-year high vulnerable to erosion — 20.5% EBITDA margin achieved in 2024 now faces compression from tax pass-through limits, creating urgency to protect profitability through planning excellence.
