Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Nike faces acute margin pressure with 130bps gross margin compression and $1.5bn tariff costs, creating urgent need for supply chain optimization. The company is in mid-turnaround under CEO Elliott Hill with clear mandate to restructure operations, improve inventory health, and diversify sourcing away from China. This represents a high-urgency, high-value opportunity for Accenture to deploy integrated planning transformation across procurement, supply chain network design, and margin recovery.

Signals to Lead With

→ Structural margin crisis requiring intervention — Gross margin fell 130bps to 40.2% with 300bps tariff pressure; CFO states margin recovery unlikely until Q2 FY2027.
→ Major supply chain restructuring underway — Nike reducing China sourcing from 16% to high single digits by May 2026, requiring rapid network redesign and supplier negotiations.
→ Active leadership change and transformation mandate — CEO Elliott Hill implementing 'Win Now' strategy with $230m restructuring charge for supply chain and technology severance costs.
→ Multi-brand portfolio complexity under stress — Converse swung $80m from profit to loss while Greater China projects 20% Q4 decline, requiring sophisticated multi-brand planning capabilities.
