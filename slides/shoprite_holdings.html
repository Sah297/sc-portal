Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Shoprite is experiencing acute margin pressure from deflation (0.7% price inflation vs. 4.7% national rate) while executing aggressive expansion (292 stores, R9.9bn H1 cash generation). The combination of market exits (Ghana, Malawi), ongoing Blue Yonder platform presence, and visible margin compression (-50bps gross margin decline) creates an immediate window for Accenture to deliver rapid ROI through supply chain optimization and AI-driven pricing/planning capabilities.

Signals to Lead With

→ Gross margin compression in deflationary environment — Gross margin declined from 24.3% to 23.84% with selling price inflation at only 0.7% versus 4.7% national food inflation, indicating urgent need for pricing and cost optimization.
→ Rapid store expansion velocity — Opened 292 stores in FY2025 with R9.9bn operating cash in H1 FY2026, creating supply chain complexity and inventory optimization needs across expanding footprint.
→ Multi-country market exits signal optimization focus — Exited Ghana and Malawi with R5.6bn assets held for sale by June 2025, consolidating to 7 Southern African countries and requiring supply chain network redesign.
→ Digital channel growth straining operations — Sixty60 delivery sales surged 34.6% to R11.9bn in H1 FY2026, requiring omnichannel inventory optimization and real-time fulfillment capabilities at scale.

• Planning Opportunities: S&OP maturity lift to integrate demand-supply balancing across 7 African countries, addressing 0.7% inflation environment and 292-store expansion velocity.
• Platform (Blue Yonder): Blue Yonder deployment to scale omnichannel capabilities (Sixty60 delivery up 34.6%), optimize pricing in deflationary markets, and support multi-country complexity with real-time visibility.
