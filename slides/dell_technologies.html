Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Dell's COO stated he has 'never seen memory-chip costs rise this fast' with DRAM up 70% YoY and internal memos describing the rate of change as 'unprecedented.' With gross margins down 90 bps despite $95.6bn revenue growth, Dell acknowledged operating at a 'high level of proficiency' in changing prices as input costs fluctuate—a clear signal they need technology-enabled planning transformation. The company's $3.6bn inventory exposure amid these rapid cost swings creates immediate urgency for real-time cost-to-serve modeling and dynamic pricing capabilities.

Signals to Lead With

→ Leadership acknowledgment of pricing capability gap — Company publicly stated they are operating at high proficiency managing rapid price changes, indicating current manual processes at their limit with 70% cost increases.
→ Unprecedented component volatility acceleration — DRAM costs surged 70% YoY with some parts up 170%, and Dell expects another 30% increase in Q4 2025—faster than any historical precedent.
→ Margin compression despite revenue growth — Gross margin declined 90 bps to 21.7% despite revenue growing to $95.6bn, demonstrating inability to pass through costs fast enough with current systems.
→ Active o9 Solutions platform investment — Existing o9 deployment provides foundation for rapid expansion into dynamic pricing and real-time margin optimization modules without platform migration risk.
