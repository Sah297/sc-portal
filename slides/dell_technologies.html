Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Dell is in acute distress with unprecedented memory cost volatility explicitly stated by leadership, gross margins compressed 240 bps, and free cash flow down 68%. The company acknowledged operating at high proficiency changing prices as input costs rapidly change, signaling urgent need for advanced planning capabilities to automate what is currently manual and reactive.

Signals to Lead With

→ Executive acknowledgment of unprecedented cost volatility — Dell stated memory cost rate of change is unprecedented with DRAM rising 50% in FY2025 and projected 30% additional increase Q4 2025.
→ Gross margin compression under revenue growth — Despite 8.1% revenue growth to $95.6bn, gross margin declined 240 bps from 24.4% to 22.2% due to component cost pressure.
→ Free cash flow collapse indicating working capital stress — Free cash flow plummeted 68.4% from $6bn to $1.9bn in FY2025, signaling inventory and working capital inefficiency.
→ Commercial division urgency for rapid deal execution — Dell's commercial division representing 85% of client sales instructed to act quickly as ordering today does not lock current prices.
