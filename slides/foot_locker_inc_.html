Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Dick's Sporting Goods completed acquisition of Foot Locker on September 8, 2025, creating a 3,200-store global sports retail platform. The integration imperative, combined with Foot Locker's structural challenges (2.5% operating margin, 140 store closures, Nike dependency, promotional pressure), creates immediate need for supply chain transformation to capture synergies and stabilize operations. With o9 Solutions already in place as the planning platform, Accenture can rapidly deploy implementation, PaaS, and AI capabilities to drive $170m working capital release and 100+ bps margin improvement during the critical 12-18 month post-merger integration window.

Signals to Lead With

→ Post-acquisition integration in progress — Dick's Sporting Goods completed $2.5bn enterprise value acquisition on September 8, 2025, combining 2,400 Foot Locker stores with 800 Dick's stores across 20 countries requiring immediate supply chain harmonization.
→ Margin compression with improvement trajectory — Operating margin compressed to 2.5% in FY2024 but Q4 2024 showed 300 bps gross margin expansion to 29.6%, demonstrating receptivity to operational improvements and planning rigor.
→ Active inventory optimization program — Inventory decreased 6.3% year-over-year in Q3 2024 with turns at 2.8x and $1.7bn inventory base, indicating management focus on working capital efficiency during transformation.
→ Platform alignment with o9 Solutions — Foot Locker uses o9 Solutions as planning platform, enabling Accenture to leverage existing technology investment for rapid deployment of advanced planning capabilities and AI agents without platform replacement risk.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: Unified S&OP across 2,400 stores and international footprint to optimize assortment allocation, store network decisions, and demand-supply synchronization during Dick's integration.
• Platform (o9 Solutions): o9 Solutions enables integrated business planning for merged Dick's-Foot Locker entity with 3,200+ stores across 20 countries, supporting real-time demand sensing and inventory allocation.
• Planning as a Service: PaaS model accelerates post-acquisition planning transformation with rapid deployment of demand planning, assortment planning, and inventory optimization capabilities during integration.
• AI & Analytics: AI agents drive promotional effectiveness in competitive sneaker market and predict traffic patterns across mall vs. off-mall locations to optimize inventory positioning.
