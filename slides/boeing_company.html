Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Boeing represents a high-urgency, high-value opportunity driven by completion of $8.3bn Spirit integration in December 2024 and need to stabilize operations after -16.1% operating margins. The company faces simultaneous pressures of integrating 15,000 employees, managing $53.9bn debt, and ramping production under FAA oversight, creating perfect conditions for comprehensive supply chain transformation. New leadership under Kelly Ortberg and post-merger integration mandate provide strong executive sponsorship and budgetary flexibility.

Signals to Lead With

→ Post-Merger Integration in Progress — Spirit AeroSystems acquisition closed December 2024 at $8.3bn with 15,000 employees integrated, creating immediate need for unified planning architecture.
→ Severe Margin Distress — Commercial Airplanes segment reported -43.9% operating margin in Q4 2024, with full-year 2024 operating margin of -16.1% and $11.8bn net loss.
→ Cash Flow Crisis Resolved — Boeing raised $24bn in equity capital Q4 2024, increasing cash position from $10.5bn to $26.3bn, providing budget for strategic supply chain investments.
→ Production Ramp Under Regulatory Scrutiny — 737 production reached 38 per month in Q2 2025 with FAA approval to increase to 42 per month, requiring precise supplier synchronization and capacity planning.
