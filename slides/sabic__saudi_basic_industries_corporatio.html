Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

SABIC faces an existential supply chain crisis with Jubail production halted indefinitely after April 7 strike, controlling 70% of global PPE supply for electronics. With $2bn+ uninsured damage per Tadawul filing, force majeure on 5 chemical lines, and inability to estimate production restart, the company urgently needs end-to-end planning transformation to manage constrained supply allocation, alternate sourcing, and customer prioritization across its 140-country footprint while protecting $31bn revenue base.

Signals to Lead With

→ Production shutdown with no restart estimate — SABIC filed April 8 Tadawul disclosure stating 'cannot estimate return to production' with material 2026 financial impact, creating urgent need for scenario-based capacity planning.
→ 70% global market share disruption — PPE resin for printed circuit boards represents strategic chokepoint causing worldwide electronics supply chain collapse, requiring immediate allocation optimization.
→ Margin compression from overcapacity — EBITDA margin at 14.5% in Q3 2025 down 4% QoQ from persistent global overcapacity, requiring product mix and pricing optimization to protect profitability.
→ Force majeure across multiple product lines — Five chemical lines (MMA, MEG, DEG, styrene, methanol) under force majeure since March 26-27 valued at $800m quarterly exports, needing supply network redesign.
