Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

RTX faces an acute operational crisis with the $3.5bn GTF recall requiring 600-700 incremental shop visits, 350+ grounded aircraft annually, and $1.1-1.3bn annual compensation payments through 2025. Simultaneously, $13.4bn in inventory and $17.1bn contract assets reveal severe working capital inefficiency, with CFO explicitly targeting $1.3bn improvement in 2025. The company is on Kinaxis RapidResponse, creating a warm technical handoff for Accenture's planning transformation and AI services to address multi-tier supplier constraints, MRO capacity bottlenecks, and margin recovery imperatives.

Signals to Lead With

→ Public commitment to $1.3bn working capital improvement — CFO stated on Jan 28, 2025 earnings call that $1.3bn WC improvement is baked into 2025 guidance with additional runway beyond, signaling executive mandate for supply chain transformation.
→ Active GTF recall with $1.1-1.3bn annual cash drain — RTX paid $1bn in GTF compensation in 2025 and projects $1.1-1.3bn in 2025, with 300-360 day MRO turnaround times versus 60 days planned, creating urgent need for planning capability.
→ Structural casting and isothermal forging supply constraints — CEO cited on Q4 2024 call that structural castings and isothermal forgings remain critical supply chain bottlenecks limiting commercial and defense production ramp, requiring supplier collaboration tools.
→ Airbus damages claim filed March 2026 — Airbus launched formal damages claim against Pratt & Whitney over GTF delivery failures stranding hundreds of completed airframes, escalating legal and commercial pressure for performance improvement.
