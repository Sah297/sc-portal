Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

The August 2024 lockout that cost $55-63m daily exposed CPKC's vulnerability to workforce disruptions and demonstrated critical need for resilient supply chain planning. With 20,000 route miles, CPKC requires advanced scenario modeling and real-time network optimization to prevent future service failures that impact grain exports, fertilizer, and chemicals. The company's existing Blue Yonder platform and $2.9bn capital investment in 2025 signal readiness for expanded planning capabilities.

Signals to Lead With

→ Massive disruption cost quantified — The lockout resulted in $55-63m daily losses for fertilizer alone and halted 40,000 carloads per day, creating urgency for resilience planning.
→ Repeated labor instability pattern — Four consecutive labor negotiations (2012, 2015, 2018, 2022) resulted in work stoppages, indicating systemic workforce planning gaps.
→ Existing Blue Yonder platform — CPKC already uses Blue Yonder, making platform expansion and AI analytics integration a natural adjacency sale with lower implementation risk.
→ Major capital investment cycle — CPKC's $2.9bn capex in 2025 and 100 new locomotives demonstrate financial capacity and appetite for operational transformation investments.
