Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Evonik presents a compelling buying signal with Q1 2026 results showing simultaneous margin compression (-90 bps), volume declines (-2%), pricing pressure (-1%), and working capital deterioration (€100m outflow) despite meeting EBITDA targets through short-term customer stockpiling. The company downgraded FY2025 guidance from €2.0-2.3bn to €1.9bn EBITDA and flagged Q2 2026 as likely peak quarter before expected H2 demand softening, creating urgency for structural planning improvements.
