Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

YPF's 90% profit collapse in Q2 2024, $799m net loss in FY2025, and persistent negative free cash flow ($824m in 9M24) signal urgent operational distress requiring immediate supply chain intervention. The company is trapped between declining conventional production (10% drop), volatile working capital swings ($328m decline in Q2 alone), and heavy capex commitments exceeding $5bn annually, creating a perfect storm for transformation investment. This distress combined with margin recovery potential (gross margin improved 55.7% YoY to 27.9%) indicates leadership will prioritize operational efficiency and planning modernization to stabilize cash generation.

Signals to Lead With

→ Earnings collapse with operational root causes — Net profit dropped 90% in Q2 2024 to $58m, then turned to $799m loss in FY2025, driven by production decline and planning inefficiencies—not just market prices.
→ Chronic negative free cash flow — Negative FCF of $824m in 9M24 despite $3.8bn adjusted EBITDA shows poor working capital and capex planning discipline, requiring integrated planning intervention.
→ Working capital volatility creating liquidity stress — Working capital declined $328m in Q2 2024 alone from procurement timing mismatches and deferred imports, indicating lack of integrated demand-supply planning.
→ Production-refining imbalance driving external purchases — 10% conventional oil decline forcing higher third-party crude purchases at $827m lifting costs (up 10.9% YoY), indicating need for supply network optimization.
