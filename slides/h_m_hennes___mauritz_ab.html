Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

H&M is at a critical inflection point in early 2026, having completed heavy supply chain investments through 2025 but now shifting focus to technology infrastructure with $1bn capex planned. The company is actively consolidating its 800-factory supplier base while moving production from China (29.5% exposure) to nearshoring hubs to mitigate tariffs, creating massive complexity. With inventory still at 56 days (vs Zara's 15-20) and fragmented supply chains causing quality/delay risks despite margin improvements, H&M needs immediate planning transformation to operationalize its sourcing restructuring and capture the full value of its supply chain investments.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active transformation pivot Q1 2026 — CEO confirmed in January 2026 shift from supply chain to technology infrastructure focus with $1bn capex, creating implementation window for planning platforms.
→ Supplier consolidation underway — Company reducing to ~800 factories across 23 countries while moving from China (230 factories, 29.5%) to Turkey/Morocco/Egypt, requiring network optimization capabilities.
→ Margin gains but inventory lag — Gross margin improved 220bps to 53.4% in FY2025 but inventory still 56 days vs Zara's 15-20, showing planning gap despite supply chain investments.
→ Tariff exposure quantified — H&M sourcing from 230+ China factories creates 15-25% tariff exposure; CFO stated need to 'increase pace to find alternative suppliers' urgently in Q2 2025.
