Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

H&M is actively transforming its supply chain with nearshoring investments and just announced a strategic pivot from supply chain to tech/stores for 2026 capex ($1bn). The company continues struggling with 17.2% inventory-to-sales ratios, persistent delivery issues, and flat margins despite 2-year transformation efforts. With $4B historical excess inventory and ongoing fulfillment problems, H&M needs planning capabilities urgently to operationalize its nearshoring strategy and capture the margin improvements it has invested heavily to enable.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active supply chain transformation underway — H&M invested SEK 11.6bn in FY24-FY25 focused on supply chain and logistics with nearshoring reducing lead times to 5-6 weeks and inventory composition described as 'good' but still at 17.2% of sales.
→ Strategic pivot creates planning capability gap — Company announced shift of 2026 capex focus from supply chain to stores/tech infrastructure (SEK 9-10bn) creating immediate need for planning capabilities to operationalize recent supply chain investments.
→ Persistent inventory and fulfillment issues — Despite transformation efforts, stock-in-trade remains elevated at SEK 37.9bn (16.4% of sales in Q3 FY25) with ongoing customer complaints about delivery failures and order fulfillment problems.
→ Margin improvement stalling despite investment — Gross margin flat at 53.4% for FY25 vs FY24 with operating margin at 8.1% vs 10% long-term target, indicating planning and optimization capabilities needed to capture value from nearshoring.
