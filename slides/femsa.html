Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

FEMSA faces a perfect storm: CEO explicitly warned of challenging 2025 consumer environment in Mexico, OXXO saw traffic decline -2.8% in Q4 2024 and same-store sales turned negative (-0.4%) in Q2 2025, while $41bn MXN in IEPS tax increases will hit beverage costs through 2026. Despite strong gross margin expansion (+130bps to 41.1%), the company must urgently transform planning to protect profitability amid declining soft drink and alcohol sales. This acute, quantified distress combined with 24,462 stores and $46.7bn revenue creates a high-value planning transformation window.

Signals to Lead With

→ CEO warning on consumer challenges — CEO stated 'increasingly challenging consumer environment in many of our markets, particularly Mexico' at start of 2025, signaling strategic planning urgency.
→ Traffic and sales reversals — OXXO store traffic declined -2.8% in Q4 2024 and same-store sales turned negative -0.4% in Q2 2025 despite 6.6% ticket increases, indicating volume crisis.
→ Material tax cost impact — IEPS tax increases will cost $41bn MXN aggregate across fiscal 2025-2026, creating immediate need for price optimization and demand planning to offset margin compression.
→ Beer and beverage category declines — Q4 2024 results showed decreases in beer and groceries categories at OXXO, directly impacting the Thirst and Gathering consumption occasions that drive retail mix.
