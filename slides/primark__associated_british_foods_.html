Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Primark presents an exceptional buying signal for Accenture with a public profit warning, documented excess inventory crisis, and imminent demerger creating urgent planning transformation needs. The company explicitly acknowledged inventory management failures requiring significant markdowns, while simultaneously preparing to operate as standalone entity post-demerger before end of 2027, creating a compressed window for supply chain reinvention.

Signals to Lead With

→ Public profit warning with inventory distress — January 2026 profit warning explicitly cited significantly increased markdowns to manage inventory levels, with 170bps margin compression and 18% profit decline in H1 2026.
→ Demerger transformation complexity — Board approved demerger of Primark from Food business in April 2026, creating urgent need for standalone planning systems and capabilities before end of 2027 calendar year.
→ Regional demand planning failure — Continental Europe (49% of sales) like-for-like sales declined 5.7% in 16 weeks to Jan 2026 while UK gained share, indicating demand sensing and allocation planning gaps.
→ Executive mandate for transformation — New CEO Eoin Tonge appointed with mandate to drive Europe turnaround and digital initiatives, with CFO also new appointee, creating receptive C-suite for planning transformation.
