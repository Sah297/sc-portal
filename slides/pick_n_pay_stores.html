Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Pick n Pay is in active crisis mode with technical insolvency, debt covenant breaches, and R4bn rights issue completed Nov 2024 making this an extremely high-urgency transformation opportunity. The company reported headline loss of 136.6c per share in H1 FY25 with trading losses up 9.1% to R718.9M, has pledged 100% of Boxer as collateral, and needs to demonstrate viability of core supermarket business ahead of Boxer IPO. New CEO Sean Summers returned specifically to execute turnaround and explicitly acknowledged falling behind on data/planning capabilities versus Shoprite. The R6.1bn debt at 6.3x EBITDA combined with ongoing losses creates burning platform for supply chain transformation investment.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active turnaround with new CEO mandate — Sean Summers appointed Jan 2024 specifically to execute turnaround after R3.2bn loss FY24, bringing external credibility for major transformation investments.
→ Completed R4bn recapitalization provides funding — R4bn rights issue completed Nov 2024 (oversubscribed) provides capital for transformation investments while Boxer IPO targeting R6-8bn creates urgency to fix core business.
→ Debt covenant breach creates forcing function — Breached all debt covenants with 6.3x EBITDA (threshold 2.75x) and had to pledge 100% Boxer shareholding as additional collateral, creating existential pressure for operational fixes.
→ Margin compression quantified and worsening — Gross margin declined from 19.6% FY23 to 18.1% FY24 to 18.4% FY25 with trading losses increasing 9.1% to R718.9M in H1 FY25 despite modest recovery efforts.

How Accenture Can Help

• Planning Opportunities: S&OP transformation to link promotional planning, demand forecasting and inventory management to stop margin erosion and reduce working capital by R4-5bn.
• Platform (Blue Yonder): Blue Yonder platform reinvention to enable demand-driven planning, promotional optimization and markdown management critical for margin recovery in distressed grocery retail.
• Planning as a Service: PaaS model ideal given major restructuring, technical insolvency and need for immediate expert-led planning capability without capex while executing R4bn rights issue.
• AI & Analytics: AI agents to optimize promotional effectiveness, reduce markdown losses and improve demand forecast accuracy which competitors like Shoprite have leveraged for market share gains.
