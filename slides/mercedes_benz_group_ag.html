Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Mercedes-Benz faces converging crises creating exceptional buying urgency: regulatory pressure from Germany's BAFA investigation on forced labour compliance with up to 2% revenue fines at stake, simultaneously with severe financial distress as margin collapsed 360bps and EBIT halved in 12 months. The company is already executing a major transformation ('Next Level Performance') targeting 10% cost reduction and capacity rationalization by 2028, creating a clear mandate for supply chain transformation with executive attention, budget allocation, and measurable ROI requirements already defined.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active regulatory investigation BAFA — ECCHR filed complaint with BAFA alleging insufficient proof of forced labour due diligence in Xinjiang supplier network; BAFA can impose fines up to 2% of global revenue (~$2.9bn exposure).
→ Executive transformation program underway — Mercedes launched 'Next Level Performance' with €3.5bn EBIT contribution in 2025 and targeting 10% unit production cost reduction by 2027 vs 2024 baseline.
→ Severe profitability deterioration — Mercedes-Benz Cars EBIT crashed 45% from €8.7bn to €4.8bn year-over-year with RoS dropping from 8.1% to 5.0%, missing double-digit target by 500bps.
→ Network rationalization commitment — Production capacity being cut to 2.2m units by 2028 with Aguascalientes plant closure in 2026, Germany capacity at 900k units, requiring planning optimization.
