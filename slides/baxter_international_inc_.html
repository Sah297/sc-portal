Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

Baxter faces an acute burning platform following Hurricane Helene's September 2024 shutdown of North Cove facility producing 60% of US IV fluids, forcing 40-60% customer allocations and exposing catastrophic single-source vulnerability. With gross margins compressed 260bps to 37.5%, elevated inventory at $2.1bn, ongoing facility recovery through year-end 2025, and new CEO search underway, Baxter requires immediate supply chain transformation to restore resilience and margin performance. The company spent 2.5M hours on recovery efforts and is actively managing global imports from 7 facilities, creating perfect timing for Accenture's planning platform and network optimization engagement.

Signals to Lead With

→ Active disaster recovery with 2.5M recovery hours invested — Company invested 2.5 million hours in hurricane recovery during 2024 with work continuing into 2025, demonstrating massive operational disruption requiring systematic supply chain redesign.
→ Leadership transition creates transformation window — Interim CEO Brent Shafer appointed in 2024 with permanent CEO search underway, creating strategic inflection point and openness to major supply chain operating model changes.
→ Margin compression creates financial urgency — Gross margin declined 260 basis points from 40.1% to 37.5% in FY2024, with hurricane costs including air freight, damaged inventory and idle facility expenses creating immediate ROI imperative.
→ Government and regulatory scrutiny on supply continuity — FDA conducted assessments, CDC issued health alerts, and HHS held briefings on Baxter's IV shortage, elevating supply chain resilience to board-level strategic priority with compliance pressure.
