Why this is a buying signal for Accenture

VW is in the midst of its largest restructuring in 87 years with 35,000-50,000 job cuts, plant closures under consideration, and operating profit down 53% in 2025. This creates an urgent 12-18 month window for supply chain transformation as the company implements €15bn cost savings while managing capacity reductions and transportation delays. The CFO publicly stated 5.9% margin is 'not sufficient' and restructuring cash outflows are embedded in 2025-2027 guidance, signaling executive sponsorship and allocated budget.

Signals to Lead With

→ Massive restructuring mandate with executive urgency — €2.6bn restructuring expenses already incurred in 2024, with CFO stating current 5.9% operating margin 'not sufficient in long run' requiring sustained cost reduction.
→ Capacity rebalancing across global footprint — 35,000 job cuts, Dresden plant closure by end 2025, Wolfsburg capacity halved, Golf production shifted to Mexico by 2027—all requiring planning transformation.
→ Working capital pressure with declining cash generation — Automotive net cash flow dropped from €10.7bn (2023) to €5bn (2024) to €2-5bn guided for 2025, with Q4 2024 showing €1.7bn improvement from working capital release.
→ Transportation and logistics bottlenecks persist — Despite semiconductor relief, company explicitly cites ongoing transportation delays and logistics challenges as distress signals impacting production flow and costs.
